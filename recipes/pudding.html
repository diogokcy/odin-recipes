Classic Brazilian Flan

Description

This Brazilian flan, known as Pudim de Leite Condensado, is a smooth, creamy dessert with a rich caramel topping. It's easy to make and perfect for special occasions or just a sweet ending to your meal. The texture is silky, similar to cr√®me caramel, and it melts in your mouth with every bite.

Ingredients

For the caramel

  • 1 cup (200g) of granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) of water

For the flan

  • 1 can (14 oz / 395g) of sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 cups (480ml) of whole milk
  • 3 large eggs

Steps

  1. Make the Caramel
    1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the sugar and water.
    2. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves.
    3. Stop stirring and let it simmer until it turns a golden amber color.
    4. Immediately pour the caramel into a round cake pan or bundt pan (about 8 inches / 20 cm).
    5. Tilt the pan to evenly coat the bottom. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Flan Mixture
    1. Preheat your oven to 350¬∞F (180¬∞C).
    2. In a blender, combine the condensed milk, whole milk, and eggs.
    3. Blend until smooth and fully mixed (about 30 seconds).
  3. Bake the Flan
    1. Pour the flan mixture over the caramel in the pan.
    2. Place the flan pan inside a larger baking dish (a water bath or bain-marie).
    3. Fill the larger dish with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the flan pan.
    4. Bake for about 60 minutes, or until the center is set but still slightly jiggly.
  4. Cool and Unmold
    1. Let the flan cool to room temperature.
    2. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best).
    3. To unmold, run a knife around the edge and invert onto a serving plate.
